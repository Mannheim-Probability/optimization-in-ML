Exercise 1 (Strict & Strong Convexity).

Prove the following statements

  1. (i)

    μ-strong convexity implies strict convexity.

    Solution.

    Assuming x≠y we have

    0<μ2⁢∥x-y∥2⁢≤μ⁢-strong conv.⁢f⁢(x)-f⁢(y)-⟨∇⁡f⁢(y),x-y⟩

    But after moving the negative parts to the left we are left with a condition which is equivalent to strict convexity by Proposition A.1.8 in the lecture. ∎

  2. (ii)

    For twice differentiable f, the following are equivalent

    1. (a)

      ∇2⁡f⁢(x)⪰μ⁢𝕀

    2. (b)

      zT⁢∇2⁡f⁢(x)⁢z≥μ⁢∥z∥2

    3. (c)

      f is μ-strongly convex

    where 𝕀 is the identity matrix and

    A⪰B:⇔A-B is (weakly) positive definite.
    Solution.

    Let us get (ii)(a)⇔(ii)(b) out of the way first:

    zT⁢∇2⁡f⁢(x)⁢z-μ⁢∥z∥2=⟨z,∇2⁡f⁢(x)⁢z⟩-⟨z,μ⁢𝕀⁢z⟩=zT⁢(∇2⁡f⁢(x)-μ⁢𝕀)⁢z

    If we have (ii)(a), then the rightmost side is positive and thus we also have (ii)(b). For the other direction we simply start from the left.

    For (ii)(a)⇒(ii)(c) let

    g⁢(y):=f⁢(y)-μ2⁢∥y∥2,

    because f is μ strongly convex

    g⁢(y) =f⁢(y)-μ2⁢∥y∥2
    ≥f(x) + ⟨∇f(x), y-x⟩+ μ2 ∥y-x∥^2 - μ2 ∥y∥^2
    =f⁢(x)-μ2⁢∥x∥2⏟=g⁢(x)+μ2⁢∥x∥2+⟨∇⁡f⁢(x)-μ⁢x⏟∇⁡g⁢(x),y-x⟩+μ⁢⟨x,y-x⟩-μ⁢⟨x,y⟩+μ2⁢∥x∥2
    =g⁢(x)+⟨∇⁡g⁢(x),y-x⟩

    g is still convex. So we know by the lecture that

    ∇2⁡g⁢(x)=∇2⁡f⁢(x)-μ⁢𝕀

    is positive definite.

    On the other hand for (ii)(c)⇒(ii)(a): if ∇2⁡f⪰μ⁢𝕀, we know that ∇2⁡g is positive definite and thus that g is convex by the lecture. Reusing the equations above except for the inequality we obtain

    f⁢(x)+⟨∇⁡f⁢(x),y-x⟩+μ2⁢∥y-x∥2-μ2⁢∥y∥2 =g⁢(x)+⟨∇⁡g⁢(x),y-x⟩
    =f⁢(y)-μ2⁢∥y∥2

    Adding μ2⁢∥y∥2 on both sides results in the definition of μ-strong convexity of f. ∎
