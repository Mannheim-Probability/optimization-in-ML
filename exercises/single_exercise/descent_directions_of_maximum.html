Exercise 1 (Descent Directions of a Maximum).

Let x*∈ℝd be a strict local maximum of f:ℝd→ℝ. Prove that every d∈ℝd is a descent direction of f in x*.

Solution.

Let ϵ>0 be such, that x* is a strict maximum in Bϵ⁢(x*)∖{x*}, where existence of such an ϵ is the strict local maximum property. We now have for any direction d that it is a descent direction, because with α¯=ϵ∥d∥>0 we have for all α∈(0,α¯]

f⁢(x*+α⁢d)<f⁢(x*),

since x*+α⁢d∈Bϵ⁢(x*)∖{x*} as ∥α⁢d∥≤α¯⁢∥d∥=ϵ. ∎
